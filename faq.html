Consultation Questions

We respond to all consultation requests within 48 hours during business days (Monday-Friday). If you submit on Friday evening or over the weekend, you'll hear from us by Tuesday at the latest. We'll contact you via your preferred method (email or phone) to schedule your consultation.

To provide the best advice, we need to know:

  • Your name, phone number, email address, and time zone
  • Your pet's name, type (cat, dog, bird, etc.), and age
  • The primary concern or question you want to address
  • Any relevant medical history or previous treatments
All fields in our consultation form are required to ensure we can provide comprehensive, personalized advice.

You can cancel or reschedule up to 24 hours before your appointment without charge. Simply contact us by phone at (555) 123-PETS or email at info@paradigm.com, and we'll help you find a new time. For cancellations with less than 24 hours notice, a $25 rescheduling fee may apply.

Currently, we only offer virtual consultations via secure video call. This allows us to serve pet owners nationwide and ensures convenience and safety for everyone. Our virtual consultations have proven to be just as effective as in-person visits for most concerns.

For multiple pets, we recommend booking individual consultations for each pet to ensure we can give each one the focused attention they deserve. However, we do offer multi-pet package pricing. Contact us directly at info@paradigm.com for information about consulting for multiple pets.

General Pet Care Questions

This varies by species and age:

  • Puppies/Kittens: Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks old for vaccinations and check-ups
  • Adult pets (1-7 years): Annual wellness exams
  • Senior pets (7+ years): Every 6 months for comprehensive check-ups
  • Birds/Exotics: Annual exams for most species, more frequent for certain conditions
Always consult with your veterinarian for a schedule tailored to your pet's specific needs.

Seek veterinary care if your pet shows any of these signs:

  • Not eating or drinking for 24+ hours
  • Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing at rest
  • Repeated vomiting or diarrhea
  • Lethargy or inability to stand/walk
  • Visible pain or discomfort
  • Sudden changes in behavior or personality
  • Bleeding from any orifice
  • Seizures or collapse
When in doubt, it's always better to err on the side of caution and consult a veterinarian.

Use these simple checks:

  • Rib Test: You should be able to feel your pet's ribs easily with light pressure, but they shouldn't be visible
  • Waist Check: When viewed from above, your pet should have a visible waist behind the ribs
  • Abdominal Tuck: When viewed from the side, the abdomen should tuck up behind the rib cage
About 60% of pets in the U.S. are overweight, which can lead to numerous health problems. Our Pexperts can help create a weight management plan during a consultation.

In an emergency:

  1. Stay calm - Your pet will pick up on your anxiety
  2. Contact your veterinarian or emergency clinic immediately
  3. Keep your pet warm, quiet, and still to prevent further injury
  4. Do not give human medications unless specifically instructed by a veterinarian
  5. Transport carefully - Use a carrier or makeshift stretcher if needed
  6. Call ahead to let the clinic know you're coming
Keep your veterinarian's phone number and the nearest emergency clinic information readily accessible.
