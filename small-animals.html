Small Animals Care Guide

Small Animal Care Essentials

Small mammals like hamsters, guinea pigs, rabbits, ferrets, and others can be wonderful pets. Each species has unique needs for housing, diet, and socialization. Our guide covers the essentials for keeping your small pet healthy and happy.

Species Comparison Guide

Species Lifespan Housing Size Social Needs Diet
Guinea Pig 5-7 years Minimum 7.5 sq ft Highly social (pairs/groups) Hay, pellets, fresh veggies
Rabbit 8-12 years Minimum 12 sq ft Social (pairs recommended) Hay, pellets, leafy greens
Hamster 2-3 years 450+ sq inches floor Solitary Commercial mix, fresh foods
Ferreet 6-10 years Multi-level cage Highly social High-protein carnivore diet
Rat 2-3 years 2+ cubic feet per rat Highly social (same-sex pairs) Commercial blocks, fresh foods

Housing & Environment

Essential Housing Requirements

  • Space: Provide adequate space for exercise and species-specific behaviors
  • Bedding: Use safe, absorbent bedding (avoid cedar/pine for most species)
  • Hideouts: Multiple hiding places for security
  • Enrichment: Toys, tunnels, chew items, and climbing opportunities
  • Temperature: Maintain 65-75°F (18-24°C) for most species
  • Cleanliness: Spot clean daily, deep clean weekly

An enriched habitat with proper bedding, hideouts, and toys

Diet & Nutrition

Herbivores

Guinea Pigs & Rabbits: Unlimited hay (80%), fresh veggies (15%), pellets (5%)

Important: Vitamin C supplementation for guinea pigs

Omnivores

Rats & Hamsters: Commercial blocks/mix (85%), fresh foods (10%), treats (5%)

Important: Protein control for rats to prevent tumors

Carnivores

Ferrets: High-protein, high-fat diet. Raw or premium ferret food only

Important: No carbohydrates or plant-based foods

Health Warning Signs

Weight Loss/Overgrown Teeth

Common in rabbits/guinea pigs. Provide unlimited hay for dental wear.

Wet Tail (Diarrhea)

Serious condition in hamsters. Requires immediate veterinary care.

Respiratory Issues

Sneezing, wheezing, nasal discharge. Common in many small pets.

Essential Tips

  • Research species thoroughly before adopting
  • Purchase from reputable breeders or adopt
  • Find an exotic vet BEFORE you need one
  • Handle gently and frequently for socialization
  • Provide daily supervised out-of-cage time
  • Never house incompatible species together

Species Highlights

  • Guinea Pigs: Need vitamin C daily, companionship
  • Rabbits: Require spay/neuter, litter training possible
  • Hamsters: Nocturnal, need large enclosures
  • Ferrets: Need vaccination, frequent playtime
  • Rats: Highly intelligent, need mental stimulation

Safe Treats

  • Herbivores: Carrot tops, bell peppers, cilantro
  • Omnivores: Cooked egg, mealworms, berries
  • All: Small amounts of fruits as occasional treats
  • Avoid: Iceberg lettuce, avocado, chocolate
