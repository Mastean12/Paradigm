Comprehensive Fish Care Guide

Fish represent a broad category of animals in the aquatic world, making up nearly half of all known vertebrate species. Whether you're considering freshwater or saltwater fish, we're here to help you create a thriving aquatic environment.

Freshwater Fish Care

Freshwater fish are the most popular choice for home aquariums, with approximately 850 species available. They're generally easier to care for than saltwater species.

Tank Setup & Maintenance

  • Tank Size: Minimum 20 gallons for most community fish
  • Filtration: Use a filter rated for 4-5 times your tank volume per hour
  • Heating: Most tropical fish require 75-80°F (24-27°C)
  • Water Changes: 25% weekly or 50% monthly
  • Cycling: Always cycle new tanks for 4-6 weeks before adding fish

A well-maintained freshwater community tank

Saltwater Fish Care

Saltwater (marine) aquariums offer incredible biodiversity but require more precise water parameters and maintenance.

Key Considerations

  • Tank Size: Minimum 30 gallons, larger is better for stability
  • Salinity: Maintain 1.023-1.025 specific gravity
  • pH Level: Keep between 8.1-8.4
  • Protein Skimmer: Essential for waste removal
  • Live Rock: Provides biological filtration and hiding places

Important Note: Saltwater aquariums require a significant investment in time, equipment, and knowledge. We recommend starting with freshwater if you're new to fish keeping.

Common Fish Health Issues

Ich (White Spot Disease)

White spots on body/fins, flashing against objects. Treat with raised temperature and medication.

Ammonia Poisoning

Red gills, gasping at surface, lethargy. Test water immediately and perform water changes.

Fin Rot

Frayed or disintegrating fins. Improve water quality and consider antibacterial treatment.

Quick Tips

  • Always acclimate fish slowly (drip method recommended)
  • Test water parameters weekly
  • Don't overfeed - feed only what fish consume in 2 minutes
  • Quarantine new fish for 2-4 weeks
  • Research fish compatibility before adding to community tanks

Essential Equipment

  • Appropriately sized aquarium
  • Quality filter system
  • Aquarium heater (for tropical fish)
  • Thermometer
  • Water test kit
  • Aquarium lighting
  • Net, gravel vacuum, algae scraper
  • Water conditioner

Beginner-Friendly Species

Freshwater: Guppies, Platies, Tetras, Corydoras, Betta (solo)

Saltwater: Clownfish, Damselfish, Blennies, Gobies
