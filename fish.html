Fish Care Guide

Freshwater and saltwater aquarium maintenance, water testing, and creating a healthy aquatic environment for your fish.

The Wonderful World of Aquarium Fish

Fish represent a broad class of aquatic animals that can live in fresh or saltwater, depending on the species. Bringing fish into your home can be an exciting hobby, especially if you intend to breed them, and the presence of a fish tank in your home can induce feelings of peace and tranquility.

Weekly Water Testing

Whether you are bringing your fish home for the first time or maintaining your aquarium, it is important to test your water once per week to ensure it is free from toxins that can harm your fish.

Testing Schedule

Weekly: pH, Ammonia, Nitrites, Nitrates
Monthly: Complete water test including hardness and alkalinity
After Issues: Test immediately if fish show signs of stress

Freshwater Fish Care

There are several freshwater fish to choose from when setting up your aquarium, but it is best to consult with a "Pexpert" to ensure your fish will get along! No matter the species, ensure your fish are suited for a freshwater environment by investing in a water testing kit.

Freshwater Water Parameters

Follow these general water chemistry guidelines to ensure your fish will adapt well to your aquarium and avoid ingesting toxins that can harm or kill your fish:

Saltwater Fish Care

Saltwater fish also offer several options when it comes to choosing among species for your aquarium. As with freshwater fish, consult with a "Pexpert" to ensure your fish will get along and are suited for a saltwater environment.

Aquarium Setup & Maintenance

Setting Up Your First Aquarium

Step 1: Choose aquarium size and location (away from direct sunlight and drafts)
Step 2: Install substrate, decorations, and equipment (filter, heater, lighting)
Step 3: Add dechlorinated water and start the nitrogen cycle (4-6 weeks)
Step 4: Test water parameters until stable
Step 5: Add fish gradually (start with hardy species)

Weekly Maintenance Routine

  • Water Testing: Check pH, ammonia, nitrites, nitrates
  • Partial Water Change: 10-25% of tank volume
  • Glass Cleaning: Remove algae from viewing panes
  • Filter Maintenance: Rinse media in tank water (not tap water)
  • Equipment Check: Ensure heater, filter, lights working properly
  • Water Conditioning: Add dechlorinator to new water

Feeding Guidelines

Frequency: Most fish should be fed 1-2 times daily
Amount: Only what they can consume in 2-3 minutes
Variety: Offer different foods (flakes, pellets, frozen, live)
Fasting: One fasting day per week helps digestive health

Making Tap Water Safe for Fish

For more information on making your tap water safe for your fish, please visit:

  • The Aquarium Wiki: Making Tap Water Safe
  • FishBase: Comprehensive Fish Database

Important: Always use a water conditioner to remove chlorine and chloramines from tap water. These chemicals are deadly to fish and beneficial bacteria in your filter.

Weekly Aquarium Checklist

  • Test water parameters
  • 10-25% water change
  • Clean aquarium glass
  • Check equipment function
  • Observe fish behavior
  • Trim live plants (if any)

Common Water Problems

  • Cloudy water (bacterial bloom)
  • Green water (algae bloom)
  • High ammonia (new tank or overfeeding)
  • Low pH (acidic water)
  • High nitrates (infrequent water changes)

Essential Equipment

  • Quality filter
  • Appropriate heater
  • Water testing kit
  • Aquarium thermometer
  • Gravel vacuum
  • Algae scraper
